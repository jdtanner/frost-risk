Greenhouse buffer: An unheated greenhouse typically stays 2–5°C warmer than outside air on a calm, clear night. This buffer shrinks on windy nights — potentially only 1–2°C when it's gusty. The estimated minimum inside is wind-adjusted. Use it to judge whether seedlings need extra protection such as fleece, bubble wrap lining, or a heated propagator.

Air frost — air temperature at 1.2 m drops to 0°C or below. Directly damaging to unprotected seedlings.

Ground frost — the ground surface reaches 0°C. Can occur when air temp is still 2–3°C, especially on still clear nights when the ground radiates heat rapidly. Wind significantly suppresses ground frost risk.

Hoar frost — ice crystals form on surfaces when dewpoint falls below 0°C and wind is calm. Calculated from temperature and humidity. Indicates a very cold, still night.

Dewpoint — the temperature at which moisture in the air would condense. A sub-zero dewpoint indicates hoar frost potential.
